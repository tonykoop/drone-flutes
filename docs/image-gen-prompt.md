# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single finished Native American style drone flute photographed as a real physical object, lying on its side at a three-quarter product-photography angle, ~50mm lens. The body is solid black walnut with a deep chocolate grain, machined into a faceted octagonal cross-section that runs the full length of the long, slender pipe. A separate bird block sits saddled across the top facet, lashed down with a thin natural leather lace. The two open ends of the open-open pipe are clearly visible. Geometric Broinwood-style inlay in pale hard maple is set flush into the flat facets — chevron bands near the ends, a diamond band mid-body, and a constellation-and-arrow feature panel — bright maple against dark walnut. At the foot end, a turned drone block with a round tenon plugs into a socket, and two additional swappable drone blocks rest just behind it. Hand-rubbed satin oil finish, soft wood sheen. Clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, realistic scale cues suggesting an arm's-length instrument, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same faceted walnut-and-maple drone flute staged in a warm woodworking shop on a linen drop, bird block lashed on top, with its three swappable drone blocks fanned out in front. Natural window light raking across the octagonal facets and lighting the pale maple inlay against the dark walnut. Wood shavings, a few hand tools, and a softly blurred lathe in the background tell the maker's-bench story. Shallow depth of field, environmental storytelling, concept visualization only, no text.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the signature octagonal-facet body with flush geometric maple inlay set into black walnut — sharp chevron and diamond bands and the constellation-and-arrow feature panel, the crisp angled edges where flat facets meet, and the swappable drone block's turned tenon entering its socket at the foot. Raking light to bring out tool-crisp facet edges, the contrast between bright hard maple and dark walnut grain, and the satin oil finish. High detail, photorealistic, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text/logos/watermarks/fake maker marks, no dimension callouts or measurement lines, no impossible mechanisms or floating parts, no rounded one-piece round body (it is faceted/octagonal), no holes drilled into the inlay; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
